Frequently Asked Questions

Question Answer
1. What are the common causes of back pain? Back pain can result from poor posture, muscle strain, herniated discs, arthritis, or sedentary lifestyle. A professional evaluation helps find the exact cause.
2. When should I see a physiotherapist for back pain? If your back pain lasts more than a week, worsens over time, or limits your daily activities, it’s best to consult a back pain physiotherapist for timely care.
3. Can physiotherapy prevent back surgeries? Yes, early physiotherapy often strengthens your back muscles, improves flexibility, and may prevent the need for surgical intervention in many cases.
4. What therapies are used for back pain treatment? Treatment may include manual therapy, core strengthening exercises, posture correction, traction therapy, electrotherapy, and ergonomic advice customized to your needs.
5. How long does it take to recover from back pain with physiotherapy? Recovery time varies. Mild cases improve within 2–4 weeks, while chronic back issues may require a few months of consistent therapy and lifestyle changes.
6. Can I continue working while getting back pain physiotherapy? In most cases, yes. Your physiotherapist will guide you on safe movements, sitting posture, and adjustments at work to support your healing.
7. Do you provide ergonomic advice for back pain prevention? Absolutely. Along with therapy, we educate patients on proper sitting, lifting, and sleeping postures to prevent future back pain episodes.
8. Is home physiotherapy available for severe back pain? Yes, we offer home visits for patients unable to travel due to severe back pain. Personalized therapy at home ensures comfort and faster recovery.
