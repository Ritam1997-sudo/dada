What is Hijama and How Does It Work?

Illustration of hijama cups being applied during therapy

Hijama, also known as wet cupping, is a traditional therapy where small cups are placed on the skin to create suction. This suction draws blood to the surface, and in wet cupping, tiny incisions are made to remove stagnant blood. Cupping therapy in Kolkata can be dry (without incisions) or wet, depending on your needs.

The process works by:

  • Improving blood circulation to targeted areas
  • Releasing toxins and waste from the body
  • Stimulating the body’s natural healing processes

In Kolkata, hijama is often used to treat pain, stress, and chronic conditions. Many Kolkata hijama centers combine traditional techniques with modern hygiene standards to ensure a safe experience.

Types of Cupping Therapy Available

Not all cupping therapies are the same. In Kolkata, you’ll find several types, each with unique benefits:

Dry Cupping

Uses suction without incisions to improve circulation and relieve muscle tension.

Wet Cupping (Hijama)

Involves small incisions to remove stagnant blood, ideal for detoxification.

Fire Cupping

Uses heat to create suction, commonly used for deep tissue relief.

Massage Cupping

Combines cupping with massage for relaxation and pain relief.

Most Kolkata hijama centers offer these options, allowing you to choose based on your health goals.

What to Expect During a Hijama Session

Curious about what happens during hijama in Kolkata? Here’s a step-by-step guide:

  1. Consultation: The practitioner discusses your health concerns and goals.
  2. Preparation: The skin is cleaned, and cups are placed on specific points.
  3. Suction: Suction is created using a pump or fire, depending on the method.
  4. Incisions (Wet Cupping): For hijama, small incisions are made to draw out blood.
  5. Aftercare: The area is cleaned, and you’re given tips to care for the skin.

Sessions typically last 20-60 minutes, and you may feel relaxed or slightly sore afterward. Most Kolkata hijama centers provide a comfortable environment to ease first-time clients.

Safety and Precautions

While cupping therapy in Kolkata is generally safe, there are precautions to keep in mind:

Who Should Avoid Hijama?

  • Pregnant women
  • People with bleeding disorders
  • Those with skin infections or open wounds
  • Individuals on blood thinners

Always consult a doctor before trying hijama in Kolkata, especially if you have chronic conditions. Choose a certified Kolkata hijama center to minimize risks.
