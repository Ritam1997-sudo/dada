FAQ
Frequently Asked Questions
How does the image scraper work? ▼
ImageHarvest fetches the target webpage HTML via a CORS proxy, then parses all image references — including img src, srcset, CSS background-image, data-src, and picture elements — entirely in your browser. No images or data are ever stored on our servers.
What are keywords used for? ▼
When you download images, they are renamed using your keywords in rotation. For example, if your keywords are "red shoes" and "nike sneakers", the images will be saved as red-shoes-1.jpg, nike-sneakers-1.jpg, red-shoes-2.jpg, etc. This is ideal for SEO — search engines index image filenames as part of relevance ranking.
Why do some websites not work? ▼
Some websites block CORS proxy requests, require authentication (login), or use JavaScript rendering to load images dynamically. The scraper works best on publicly accessible, server-rendered pages. JavaScript-heavy SPAs (React/Vue apps) may only show partially loaded images.
How many images can I extract and download? ▼
There is no hard limit. The Max Images setting defaults to 500 per URL but can be raised to 2000. In Bulk Mode you can process 20 URLs simultaneously, potentially extracting thousands of images in one session.
Can I use this for commercial purposes? ▼
ImageHarvest is a technical extraction tool. You are solely responsible for ensuring you have the legal right to download and use any images you extract. Always respect copyright, website terms of service, and applicable laws before using downloaded images commercially.
What is the naming pattern? ▼
The default pattern is keyword-N.ext — e.g., blue-sneakers-1.jpg. You can change this in Advanced Settings to keyword_originalname, sequential numbers, or keep original filenames. Spaces in keywords are replaced with hyphens for web-safe filenames.
